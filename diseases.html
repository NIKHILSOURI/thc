Nutrition

In developing countries, over 80 percent lack access to basic medical procedures, drugs, and vaccines. Complementary and alternative practices are popular among wealthier populations globally, despite limited evidence of their safety and effectiveness. Ayurvedic research is gaining traction, both in India and internationally . The National Center for Complementary and Alternative Medicine in the US leads scientific research in this field, aiming to explore these practices with rigorous science, support research, and disseminate information . Complementary and alternative practices often serve as adjuncts or alternatives to Western medicine, influenced by economic factors. While cost may initially drive interest, studies suggest other factors such as confidence, access, and convenience are equally significant. Traditional medicine isn't solely utilized by the poor; individuals from all socioeconomic backgrounds seek it for various reasons, including perceived lower side effects. Moreover, users of complementary methods often also seek conventional care .
